Coconut Curry Chicken

This coconut curry chicken can be made in one pot and is the perfect weeknight dinner! It is super easy to make, yet exotic enough to make it feel like take-out.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless-skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 3 cloves minced garlic
  • 2 tbsp finely minced ginger
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tsp yellow curry powder
  • 3 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 3 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 large red bell pepper
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 can sliced water chestnuts
  • 1 can pineapple chunks (optional)
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 tsp fish sauce (optional)
  • 1/4 cup cilantro and/or basil, diced
  • fine sea salt and freshly cracked pepper
  • Serve over/with: cooked basmati rice, naan bread, lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Prep all the ingredients: mince the garlic and ginger, dice the onion, slice the red bell pepper.
  2. Heat 2 tbsp coconut oil in a large, deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and saute for 3-5 minutes or until they turn golden.
  3. Add the ginger and garlic. Stir to coat everything with the coconut oil.Reduce heat to low, add the red curry paste, curry powder, and coriander. Stir frequently for 2-3 minutes, or until the dish is fragrant and lightly toasted.
  4. Return the heat to medium-high. Add the remaining 1 tbsp of coconut oil and the red pepper. Stir for a few minutes, then add the bite-sized chicken pieces.
  5. Add salt and pepper to taste. (I add 1 tsp sea salt and 1/2 tsp of pepper.) Cook the chicken, stirring frequently for 4-5 minutes until the chicken is browned on each side, not fully cooked.
  6. Add brown sugar, coconut milk, water chestnuts, pineapple chunks, lime juice, and fish sauce.
  7. Let simmer for ten minutes to ensure chicken is fully cooked through. This also allows the flavors to combine and the curry sauce to thicken.
