Pork Chive Dumplings

Ingredients

  • 12 oz (1 1/2 lbs) of ground pork
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp minced raw ginger
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 small bunch of scallions divided
  • 3 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp Chinese five spice (optional)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pk round wonton/dumpling wrappers

Instructions

  1. Take half of a bunch of scallions and chop completely (whites and greens). Set the other half aside for use as a garnish. Add the sesame oil to a pan and saute over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the garlic, ginger, and chopped scallions. Cook until fragrant for about 1 minute to remove the raw taste from the ingredients withoug cooking them. Remove from heat and set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, hoisin and soy sauces, Chinese five spice, and the egg. Mix until uniform.
  3. Place about 1 1/2 tsp of the pork mixture into the center of each dumpling. Brush a little water around the edge of the wonton with your finger. Seal the dumpling shut by pinching the sides together, making sure to get all the excess air out.
  4. Once the dumpling is shut and sealed, create the pleats using your thumb and forefinger. Repeat with the rest of the wonton wrappers
  5. Heat a pan over medium-high heat with oil. Place dumplings into hot oil and fry for 1-2 minutes. Remove and drain over paper towels.
  6. Place dumplings back into pan and add 1/2 cup of wate so that ir rises up the dumplings about halfway. Cover and steam for 5-7 minutes.
  7. Use the remaining chopped scallions as a garnish, and serve immediately with your favorite sauce. Enjoy!
