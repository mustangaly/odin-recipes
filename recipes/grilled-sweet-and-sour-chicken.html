Grilled Sweet and Sour Chicken

To help add some variety to the dry-rubbed grilled selections we normally have, this saucy, healthy spin on a takeout classic is sure to spice up your recipe book.

Ingredients

  • 8-12 skinless, boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp canola oil
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 small can of mandarin oranges, half the juice drained, oranges smashed
  • 3 Tbsp corn starch
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • salt and pepper
  • dash of red pepper flakes

Instructions

For the Chicken:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375° F.
  2. Make sure the skin has been romoved from the chicken and season both sides with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat a grill pan over high heat and add a drizzle of oil.
  4. Add the chicken, top down, and cook for 1 minute or until grill marks appear.
  5. Turn the pan down to medium and cook for 10 minutes.
  6. Move the chicken to the oven and bake for another 10-15 minutes or until juices run clear.
  7. For boneless chicken thighs (as opposed to bone-in), cook the thighs in a pan ove medium-high heat for 4 minutes on each side, basting with the sauce after you turn them over the first time, and then once more before serving.

For the Sauce:

  1. In a large pan over medium heat, cook the canola oil, sugar, onion powder, rice vinegar,ketchup, and soy sauce together until everything is dissolved and uniform.
  2. Add the garlic and the mandarin ornages, cook for 2 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the cornstarch and the water
  4. Add a little of the hot mixture to the bowl and then slowly whisk the cornstarch mixture into the pan.
  5. Stir continuously for another minute and let simmer for a few minutes or until thickened.
  6. Store in an air-tight container in the fridge if any remains leftover.
