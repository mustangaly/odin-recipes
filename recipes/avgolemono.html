Avgolemono Soup

A deliciously creamy Greek chicken soup with dill, lemon, and eggs.

Avgolemono is one of my favorite things to make because it is super nutritious, relatively inexpensive, and easy to put together!

Ingredients

  • 6 chicken breasts, whole
  • 2-3 carrots, cut into 1-inch slices
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups of rice
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 cup of lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup of minced fresh dill
  • Dill sprigs (for garnish)
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Instructions

  1. Fill a stock pot half to two-thirds of the way with water. Place the chicken breasts, minced dill, and 2-3 bay leaves in the pot, cover, and simmer for one hour.
  2. While the chicken is cooking, cut all the vegetables.
  3. Take the chicken breasts out of the pot. Set aside to shred after they cool for several minutes.
  4. Bring the remaining broth to a boil, add salt to taste. Add the vegetables and rice, cover, and simmer for 20 mins.
  5. While the rice is cooking, shred the chicken. If you have a Kitchen Aid, the dough mixer attachement works perfectly for this.
  6. Beat the eggs, add the lemon juice slowly while stirring. The lemon juices acts to prime the egg-whites to help prevent curdling.
  7. Take two cups of broth from the pot and SLOWLY add to the egg/lemon mixture while stirring constantly. This heats the eggs up slowly so it won't curdle when you add it into the soup.
  8. When the eggs and the broth are well-incorporated, pour the mixture back into the broth while stirring.
  9. Add the shredded chicken, and allow the soup to simmer for a couple of minutes so the flavors can combine.
  10. Serve in bowls, garnish with fresh ground pepper and dill sprigs. Enjoy!
